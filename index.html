Naval Ravikant

The man who inspired thousands of young entrepeneours.

Naval Ravikant is an entrepreneur, angel investor, philosopher of wealth, and one of the most influential voices in the startup and tech world. Born in India and raised in New York, Naval built his career in Silicon Valley, where he co-founded AngelList in 2010, a platform that completely changed how startups raise capital and connect with investors. Beyond business, Naval is known for his timeless thoughts on wealth, happiness, and self-improvement, often shared through podcasts, interviews, and his famous tweetstorms.

Naval's contribution in the start-up space:

  • Before AngelList, raising capital was limited to a few elite circles in Silicon Valley. Naval opened up startup funding to thousands of founders globally, allowing anyone with drive and vision to pitch their ideas, not just those with the right connections. Introduced syndicates, which let individual investors pool resources, empowering young founders to get funding faster.
  • Naval personally invested early in companies like Twitter, Uber, Yammer, Postmates, and Notion. By backing founders at the idea stage, he set an example: great investors bet on people, not just metrics, a philosophy that gave countless young founders confidence.
  • His tweetstorm “How to Get Rich (Without Getting Lucky)” became a modern manifesto for entrepreneurs. It gave a framework for young, ambitious people to understand leverage (code, capital, media) and the value of specific knowledge. Through podcasts and essays, he shared mental models on compounding, decision-making, and building enduring wealth, lessons usually reserved for insiders.
  • Naval emphasized that startups aren’t just about chasing money, they’re about freedom, creativity, and personal leverage. He shifted the mindset for thousands of young builders, showing that it’s possible to combine capitalism with philosophy, and success with peace of mind.
  • AngelList jobs board opened the startup world to young talent worldwide, not just founders, connecting ambitious people with high-growth startups. This gave first-time operators and future founders a stepping stone into tech, lowering barriers to entry.
